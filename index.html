How it works

Deliberate practice
for difficult conversations

The training market has been selling awareness for twenty years. Candor builds what comes after it: the capacity to act differently when the conversation gets hard.

01

Choose your scenario

Select from a library of 10 conversation types: underperformance, conflict mediation, promotion denial, retention, layoff, and more. Each scenario is calibrated to the level of difficulty you are ready for.

02

Practice with AI or an actor

AI sessions are available anytime. Actor sessions pair you with a trained professional who responds to what is actually in the room, not a script. You cannot perform your way through it.

03

Get your behavioral feedback

After every session, Candor's AI analyzes what you actually did under pressure. Not a score. A behavioral reflection: when you stayed, when you backed away, and what to try differently next time.

This is not coaching. This is deliberate practice.

Underperformance Feedback — Session Feedback
May 8, 2026 · 28 min · AI Session · Alex Rivera
What You Did

You opened by naming the issue directly — unusual for this scenario. In prior sessions, you typically entered with a wellness check framing. This is a measurable shift.

The Moment That Mattered

At exchange 5, Jordan disclosed the scope problem. You did not fill the silence that followed. You waited. That is new. Jordan’s response shifted in quality because of it. Notice that.

Where You Backed Off

In exchange 7, after setting the Thursday expectation, you softened the close: “I’m not trying to build a case against you.” Watch whether that framing becomes a habit of pre-apologizing for clarity.

What to Try Next

Your next session: same scenario, one level harder — Jordan pushes back on the Thursday deadline. Practice sitting in the disagreement without revising the expectation downward. That is the next threshold.
