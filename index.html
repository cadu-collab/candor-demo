How it works

Deliberate practice
for difficult conversations

The training market has been selling awareness for twenty years. Candor builds what comes after it: the capacity to act differently when the conversation gets hard.

01

Choose your scenario

Select from a library of 10 conversation types: underperformance, conflict mediation, promotion denial, retention, layoff, and more. Each scenario is calibrated to the level of difficulty you are ready for.

02

Practice with AI or an actor

AI sessions are available anytime. Actor sessions pair you with a trained professional who responds to what is actually in the room, not a script. You cannot perform your way through it.

03

Get your behavioral feedback

After every session, Candor's AI analyzes what you actually did under pressure. Not a score. A behavioral reflection: when you stayed, when you backed away, and what to try differently next time. The analysis is grounded in proven frameworks for difficult conversations and leadership feedback.

This is not coaching. This is deliberate practice.

The analysis engine

Not a script
Not a decision tree

Candor's AI generates unique conversational friction for every session, adapts to each manager's specific inputs, and evaluates behavior against frameworks with decades of peer-reviewed backing.

01

Adaptive agentic AI

Every conversation is generated in real time from the manager's actual choices. The AI plays a realistic counterpart — with defensiveness, deflection, and emotional texture — calibrated to the selected difficulty level. No two sessions are identical.

02

Built on proven frameworks

The analysis engine is built on and inspired by proven frameworks around difficult conversations and leadership feedback — methodologies with decades of applied research behind them, translated into behavioral signals the AI can detect and surface in real time.

03

High precision, zero fabrication

Candor only surfaces feedback on behaviors detectable in the actual conversation. No inferred intent. No projected personality. No human evaluator subjectivity. Every observation is anchored to a specific exchange — with the moment cited.

After every session

Candor shows you exactly
what happened

An objective mirror — without the subjectivity of a manager, peer, or coach. Candor evaluates specific communication patterns against established behavioral frameworks, not impressions. What you get is what actually happened in the conversation, cited by exchange.

Underperformance Feedback — Session Feedback
May 8, 2026 · 28 min · AI Session · Alex Rivera
What You Did

You opened by naming the issue directly — unusual for this scenario. In prior sessions, you typically entered with a wellness check framing. This is a measurable shift.

The Moment That Mattered

At exchange 5, Jordan disclosed the scope problem. You did not fill the silence that followed. You waited. That is new. Jordan’s response shifted in quality because of it. Notice that.

Where You Backed Off

In exchange 7, after setting the Thursday expectation, you softened the close: “I’m not trying to build a case against you.” This is the Silence — Masking pattern: the message was delivered, then immediately softened to reduce perceived threat. The words were right. The close was not.

What to Try Next

Your next session: same scenario, one level harder — Jordan pushes back on the Thursday deadline. Practice sitting in the disagreement without revising the expectation downward. That is the next threshold.
