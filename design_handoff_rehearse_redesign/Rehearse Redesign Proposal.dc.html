Key decisions
1
Editorial hero

Mono eyebrow on Blush + a large Bricolage display headline set a confident, human tone. Ember is reserved for a single primary CTA so the action always reads first.

2
Warm stat pause

The stats sit on a full-bleed Blush band with oversized Ember figures — a warm, human beat between sections rather than a cold data strip.

3
Mono step numbers

JetBrains Mono numerals label the process steps, echoing the craft of rehearsal. Cards float on Paper with airy whitespace for calm scanning.

4
Ink pillar section

"Be more human" inverts to an Ink surface for gravity. The Practice/not-theory contrast uses Bricolage bold vs. Stone regular to hold hierarchy in two words.

5
Inviting waitlist

The form sits on a Blush panel with a Paper card — inviting rather than corporate. The Ember submit anchors conversion at the bottom of the flow.

T
Type system

Bricolage Grotesque for display, Hanken Grotesk for body/UI, JetBrains Mono for eyebrows and meta — a clear three-role hierarchy applied consistently.
